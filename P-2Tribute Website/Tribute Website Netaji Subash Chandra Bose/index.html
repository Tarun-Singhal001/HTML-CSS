S.C Bose

1897-1945

Netaji

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose (1897 - 1945) was an Indian revolutionary and nationalist leader who played a key role in the country's independence movement. He is also known as "Netaji".

Bose was born in Cuttack, Orissa (now Odisha) to Janakinath Bose and Prabhavati Devi. He graduated from the University of Calcutta's Scottish Church College in 1918 with a degree in Philosophy.

Bose was a leader of the Indian National Congress and helped organize the Non-Cooperation Movement in 1920. He is known for his leadership skills and oratory abilities, and his famous slogans include "Jai Hind", "Delhi Chalo", and "Tum mujhe khoon do, main tumhe aazadi dunga".

During World War II, Bose led an Indian national force from abroad against the Western powers. He also formed Azad Hind Fauj and made other contributions to India's freedom struggle. However, his wartime alliances with Nazi Germany and Imperial Japan have left a legacy that is complicated by authoritarianism.
